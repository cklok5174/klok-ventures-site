Built for Restaurants & Hospitality
Own the guest relationship. Cut the middlemen. Never reprint another menu.
Mezzanine puts your full menu, ordering, allergen info, and loyalty program directly in your guests' hands — no app store, no commission, no reprints. Your brand, your data, your revenue.
Pain Points This Eliminates
💸
Third-party platforms taking 15–30% per order
DoorDash, Grubhub, and UberEats extract a cut from every order placed through them, and they own the customer relationship — not you. This app routes orders directly from guest to kitchen with zero commission, and you retain the guest's data for repeat marketing.
🚨
Allergy incidents from unclear menu information
Paper menus and verbal descriptions create liability exposure when a guest misses an allergen. Every item in this app shows a clear "Contains:" tag for the top allergens — fish, dairy, gluten, tree nuts, sesame — so guests self-screen before they order, and you're protected.
🙅
Servers fielding "is that still available?" all night
When an item runs out, it takes one update to mark it 86'd in the app — instantly grayed out and unorderable for every guest at every table. No more awkward table returns, no comp requests because someone ordered something that was already gone.
🖨️
Seasonal menu reprints costing thousands per year
A typical restaurant spends $3,000–$8,000 annually on menu design and printing, plus the operational drag of sticker corrections and outdated QR codes. Update dishes, prices, and descriptions in seconds — no designer, no print shop, no delay.
🃏
Loyalty programs guests don't use because cards get lost
Punch cards in wallets get forgotten. This loyalty program lives on the guest's phone with their full visit history, point balance, and tier progress always visible. That visibility drives repeat visits — guests who can see their progress toward Gold are significantly more likely to come back.
⏱️
Slow service at peak hours losing you table turns
Guests ordering through the app send requests directly to the kitchen without waiting for a server pass. Special instructions and dietary notes arrive with the order — no transcription errors, faster firing, shorter ticket times. Two minutes per table turn adds up across a full service.
