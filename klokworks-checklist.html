Legal & Financial
0/5
▶
Open a separate business checking account Free
Keep client money separate from personal money from day one. Relay, Mercury, or Novo are free, online-only business accounts that take 10 minutes to open. Makes taxes dramatically simpler and looks professional when clients see the account name on invoices.
Set up invoicing — Wave or Stripe Free
Wave is free invoicing + accounting software. Stripe lets clients pay by card and auto-sends receipts. You want to be able to send a professional invoice within 10 minutes of closing a deal — not scramble with a PDF template.
Write a simple client contract template Free
One page. Cover: scope of work (linked to a separate spec), payment terms (50% upfront, 50% on delivery), IP ownership (transfers on full payment), what happens if they stop paying retainer, and revision limits. Use Docracy for free templates to start from.
Decide your payment terms and stick to them
Standard is 50% upfront before you write a line of code, 50% on delivery. For retainers, first month upfront. Never start work without a deposit — this filters out time-wasters and protects you if a client ghosts mid-project.
Talk to an accountant about quarterly taxes Worth it
As a self-employed consultant you'll owe quarterly estimated taxes (Jan, Apr, Jun, Sep). Set aside 25–30% of every payment you receive. One hour with a CPA saves you a surprise bill in April. Many offer free first consultations.

Tools & Daily Workflow
0/8
▶
Set up a project tracker — Notion or Trello Free
One board per client: Backlog → In Progress → Review → Done. Even with one client, having a structured board keeps scope creep visible and gives you something to show when a client asks "where are we?" Notion free tier is plenty.
Set up Loom for async client demos Free
Loom lets you record your screen + face talking through a demo. Instead of scheduling a call to walk a prospect through an app, send a 3-minute Loom. Dramatically improves response rates on cold outreach.
Set up Calendly for booking calls Free
Calendly free tier lets prospects book a 30-minute discovery call without back-and-forth emails. Put the link in your email signature and on your landing page CTA. Removes friction from the first conversation.
Create a standard project kickoff questionnaire
A Google Form with 8–10 questions: business name, industry, primary workflow to solve, existing tools they use, who will use the app, rough number of users, budget range, timeline urgency. Send this after a prospect says yes to a call — gets you 80% of what you need before the meeting.
Prepare your AI transparency talking point Must Do
Every client conversation will eventually surface this. Have a confident, rehearsed answer ready: "I use AI as a core development tool — the same way a contractor uses power tools instead of hand tools. It lets me move faster and charge you less without sacrificing quality. The expertise, judgment, and industry knowledge is still mine." Don't hide it. Lead with it as a benefit.
Add AI methodology to your website Done
The "How I Work" section on your Klok Ventures landing page already addresses this directly. Make sure prospects read it before your first call — it pre-handles the objection and frames AI as the reason your pricing is competitive, not a red flag.
Build your own AI-assisted workflow system
Document exactly how you go from client brief → working demo using AI tools. What prompts do you use? What do you review and refine yourself? What's your quality check process? A documented workflow makes you consistent across projects and is your competitive moat — nobody else has your specific combination of industry knowledge and tooling.

Getting Your First Client
0/5
▶
List 10 local businesses you already know or frequent Start Here
Your first client is almost never a cold stranger — it's a restaurant you eat at, a contractor who did work on your house, a realtor in your network. Make the list. Warm outreach converts 5–10× better than cold.
Send 5 personal outreach messages with a relevant demo link
Not a mass email — a personal text or DM. "Hey [name], I built a demo app for [their industry] — thought you might find it interesting. Happy to show you what it could look like for [their business name]." Link to the relevant demo. Five messages is enough to start.
Offer your first client a discounted "founding client" rate
20–30% off your normal quote in exchange for a testimonial and permission to use their project as a portfolio piece. Explicitly state it's a limited-time rate for your first build. This removes price friction and gets you a real project under your belt.
Document the first project as a case study
Before → after. What was the problem, what you built, what changed. Even a paragraph and two screenshots is enough. This becomes your most persuasive sales tool for the second client in that industry.
Ask for a referral after delivery — explicitly
"Do you know anyone else who runs a [restaurant / construction company / etc.] who might find this useful?" Most happy clients will refer you if you ask directly. Most won't think to do it unprompted. Ask within a week of delivery while the excitement is fresh.
